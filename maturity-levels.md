# Maturity Levels

**Open Ecosystem Collective – 5 Roles, 5 Levels, One Path**

The collective has no hierarchy. It has maturity levels.  
Like open source projects: Contributor → Committer → Maintainer → Technical Lead → Foundation Member.

You grow by doing. Not by waiting.

When a project is complete, the next company has the opportunity to work with the collective.  
If a client needs further support, they request a new mandate.

`70% Network Member · 20% VRC · 10% Lua Chea`

- **70%** goes to the person executing the mandate
- **20%** goes to VRC (invoicing, contract management, payment risk — payout after client payment received)
- **10%** goes to Lua Chea (mentoring, community building, methodology infrastructure)

## Level 1 – Discovery Specialist

> *The person who opens doors.*

### What you do

You lead the first conversation with a potential client. You map their technology
stack, identify which software vendors they work with, and surface where partner
opportunities exist that they have not yet seen. You do not need to know everything.
You need to be genuinely curious and structured in how you listen.

### Core strengths for this role

* Curiosity – asking good questions and actually listening to the answers
* Fast contextual understanding – grasping a new domain quickly
* Structured note-taking – turning conversations into usable information
* Comfort with "I don't know" – and knowing how to find out

### What you deliver

**Discovery Report** – covering:

* Technology stack and vendor relationships
* Existing partnerships (formal and informal)
* Initial MDF opportunity scan
* Recommended next step (further assessment or direct quick win)

### Typical engagement

1 day on-site or remote · CHF 1'200 · Report delivered within 3 business days

### What Level 1 is not

Level 1 is not junior. It is specialised. A great Discovery Specialist
opens doors that no one else knew existed. Some people stay at Level 1 by
choice – and that is the right decision if it is where your strongest contribution lies.

## Level 2 – Assessment Lead

> *The person who maps the territory.*

### What you do

You conduct the full Partner Ecosystem Maturity Assessment – all five dimensions,
scoring, MDF-potential calculation, and the final presentation to the client.
You translate what you find into a clear, honest report that the client can act on.
You are comfortable presenting findings that the client may not want to hear.

### Core strengths for this role

* Structured analysis – working through a framework systematically
* Communication at C-level – presenting findings to CEO and CTO without losing them
* MDF knowledge – understanding vendor partner programmes in enough detail to identify real opportunities
* Courage in reporting – writing what is true, not what is comfortable

### What you deliver

**Partner Ecosystem Assessment Report** – 8–12 pages covering:

* Scores across all 5 dimensions (max 75 points)
* MDF potential in CHF/EUR/USD with specific vendors named
* 3–5 prioritised opportunities
* Quick wins for the next 30 days
* Optional follow-on engagement recommendation

Plus a 30-minute presentation to the client leadership team.

### Typical engagement

2 days · CHF 2'800 · Report delivered within 5 business days of Day 1

> The hourly rate for the Maturity Assessment (CHF 175/h) is derived from this day rate  
> (CHF 1'400 / 8h = CHF 175/h). See [pricing.md](./pricing.md) for full assessment pricing.

### Prerequisite for advancement to Level 2

* At least 3 completed Level 1 Discovery mandates documented
* One peer-reviewed case study published (anonymised)
* Demonstrated familiarity with at least 5 major vendor partner programmes
  (e.g. AWS, Google Cloud, HashiCorp, Red Hat, SUSE, ...)

## Level 3 – Collaboration Architect

> *The person who builds bridges between companies.*

### What you do

You design collaboration structures. You identify which companies should work
together, how they should structure a co-sell relationship, and what a realistic
partner roadmap looks like for the next 12 months. You have seen enough deals
to know what makes collaboration work and what makes it fail.

### Core strengths for this role

* Systems thinking – seeing how companies, products, and people connect
* Network understanding – knowing the Cloud Native landscape well enough to spot natural partnerships
* Strategy development – turning observations into structured, actionable plans
* Facilitation – comfortable running conversations between companies who do not yet trust each other

### What you deliver

**Collaboration Plan** – 6–10 pages covering:

* Partner shortlist (3–5 specific companies with rationale)
* Co-sell framework for each recommended partnership
* First 90-day action plan
* Template partnership agreement outline

### Typical engagement

3–4 days · CHF 4'800 – 6'400 · Delivered within 10 business days

### Prerequisite for advancement to Level 3

* At least 5 completed Level 2 Assessment mandates documented
* Two peer-reviewed case studies published
* Direct experience with at least one co-sell or formal partnership negotiation
* Peer review passed by two existing Level 3 or above members

## Level 4 – Growth Strategist

> *The person who builds the engine for scale.*

### What you do

You develop multi-year partner growth strategies. You think in OKRs, partner
tier structures, and revenue models. You help clients build the internal
capability to manage partner ecosystems without depending on external support
indefinitely. You understand that a great strategy is one the client can
actually execute.

### Core strengths for this role

* Long-term thinking – planning 12–24 months ahead with realistic milestones
* Revenue understanding – partner-originated revenue, MDF ROI, co-sell pipeline tracking
* Structural design – partner tier criteria, programme architecture, governance
* Moderation – facilitating multi-stakeholder strategy sessions

### What you deliver

**Partner Growth Plan** – 12–18 pages covering:

* Partner ecosystem vision for 24 months
* Partner tier structure with entry criteria
* OKR framework for partner team
* MDF roadmap with specific vendors and timelines
* Financial model: expected partner-originated revenue

### Typical engagement

5–6 days · CHF 9'000 – 10'800 · Delivered within 20 business days

### Prerequisite for advancement to Level 4

* At least 5 completed Level 3 mandates documented
* Demonstrated experience building or significantly improving a partner programme
* Three peer-reviewed case studies published
* Peer review passed by two existing Level 4 or above members

## Level 5 – Executive Advisor

> *The person who thinks alongside the leadership team.*

### What you do

You provide strategic partnership advisory at C-level. Project-based — not a retainer.  
You are brought in for a defined engagement: to challenge assumptions, facilitate key decisions,
and help leadership teams think through partner strategies that have multi-year consequences.  
You have the experience and the authority to say what others in the room will not say.

### Core strengths for this role

* Executive presence – comfortable in board rooms and investor conversations
* Pattern recognition – having seen enough situations to know which signals matter
* Strategic clarity – cutting through complexity to what actually needs to happen
* Honest counsel – long-term value over short-term approval

### What you deliver

Scope is defined per engagement. Typical deliverables include:

* **Executive Brief** – 2–3 pages: ecosystem status, key decisions, risk flags, OKR progress
* **Quarterly Strategic Review** – 90-minute session with leadership team
* **Partner Decision Framework** – structured input on specific strategic choices

### Typical engagement

Project-based · CHF 2'400 / day · Scope defined upfront · No retainer

> There are no long-term retainers in the OEC model. When a project is complete,
> the next company has the opportunity to work with the collective.
> If a client needs further support, they request a new mandate.

### Prerequisite for advancement to Level 5

* At least 5 completed Level 4 mandates documented
* Demonstrated C-level client relationship sustained over 12+ months
* Four peer-reviewed case studies published
* Peer review passed by Tania and at least one existing Level 5 member
* Recognised contribution to methodology or governance repository

## How progression works

Progression is not a promotion. It is a recognition of contribution that
has already happened.

```
1. Document your work
   → Completed mandates in the collective's shared record
   → At least one anonymised case study per level published on GitHub

2. Peer review
   → Two members at the same level or above review your documented work
   → They look for: quality of delivery, adherence to values, client outcome

3. Community vote
   → Lazy Consensus: proposal published to collective channel
   → 72 hours to object. Silence = agreement.
   → Objections require reasoning and an alternative

4. Recognition
   → GitHub badge updated
   → Updated rate tier applies to new mandates from that date

5. Give back
   → Every member who advances commits to mentoring
        one person at the level they just left
```

---

## Staying at a level by choice

There is no obligation to advance. Some people are exceptional at Level 1
and prefer to stay there. That is a legitimate and respected choice. The
collective needs excellent people at every level.

Compensation reflects the complexity of the work, not seniority for its own sake.

---

## What maturity levels are not

* They are not a measure of how long you have been in the collective
* They are not a measure of how much you earn
* They are not permanent – a member who consistently underdelivers
  may be asked to step back to the previous level after a peer review process
* They are not titles to put on a business card without the work behind them
