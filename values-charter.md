# Values Charter

**Open Ecosystem Collective – The Foundation**

> *A value only stands when it holds in a difficult moment.*

This charter defines who we are – not what we do.  
Anyone who wants to be part of the collective agrees to these values.  
Not with a signature. Through concrete behaviour.

## The three sources

Every value in this charter comes from one or more of three communities
that have already solved the question of how to build something sustainable
without controlling each other.

| 🌿 Lua Chea | ☁️ CNCF / Cloud Native | ⚙️ Open Source |
|---|---|---|
| Community, trust, inclusion | Trusted · Open · Neutral · Meritocracy | Community over Code · Forkable · Transparent |
| No one excluded for lack of means | Minimal viable governance | Contribution counts more than time |
| Neurodiversity as strength | Vendor-neutral decision making | Lazy Consensus · Give back |

## The 7 values

---

### 01 · Openness

> *What we know, we share. What we build, we show. What we decide, we explain.*

**Rooted in:** Lua Chea · CNCF · Open Source

The CNCF Charter states it directly: openness and transparency are foundational.
For us this means the methodology is public, prices are documented, and decisions
are visible. We do not protect knowledge to gain advantage.

**What it looks like when it is lived:**
- Methodology and templates are public (CC-BY-SA 4.0)
- Decisions are communicated in open channels
- Mistakes are named openly, not hidden
- Revenue split is documented and the same for everyone
- Pull requests and improvements are welcomed

**What it looks like when it is missing:**
- Methods protected as "intellectual property"
- Decisions made behind closed doors
- Mistakes attributed to others or quietly buried
- Prices negotiated differently per person
- Feedback deflected rather than absorbed

**Ask yourself:** Have I shared something this month that could have given me
an advantage if I had kept it?

---

### 02 · Generosity

> *We give first – knowledge, time, connections – not because we must, but because the network grows stronger when everyone gives.*

**Rooted in:** Lua Chea · Open Source

Lua Chea runs on a donation model: those who can pay, carry those who cannot.
Open source runs on contribution without guaranteed return. Both are the same
underlying belief – that giving first creates more value than extracting first.

**What it looks like when it is lived:**
- 20% of every mandate flows into the collective foundation
- Knowledge is shared before a contract is secured
- New members are actively guided, not left to find their own way
- Clients receive more than was promised
- Contacts are shared freely

**What it looks like when it is missing:**
- "That is my network" – contacts are hoarded
- Knowledge withheld until an engagement is confirmed
- New members left to figure things out alone
- Exactly what was agreed delivered – nothing more
- Every minute billed without exception

**Ask yourself:** Has someone in my environment benefited from my work
without knowing it – and am I at peace with that?

---

### 03 · Neutrality

> *We favour no technology, no vendor, no member for any reason other than quality and contribution.*

**Rooted in:** CNCF · Open Source

The CNCF Charter is explicit: no influence, no biased behaviour, no pay-to-play.
The Linux Foundation was built on this principle. Vendor neutrality is what makes
a foundation trustworthy. We apply the same standard to our recommendations,
our mandates, and our internal decisions.

**What it looks like when it is lived:**
- Clients receive honest recommendations even when they bring no benefit to the collective
- No vendor receives preferential treatment because of sponsorship or MDF size
- Mandates are assigned by competence and availability
- Feedback is the same regardless of someone's seniority
- No pay-to-play in recommendations

**What it looks like when it is missing:**
- Recommendations follow the commission, not the client's need
- Vendors with larger MDF potential are pushed harder
- Mandates go to friends instead of the most competent person
- Criticism of senior members is softened
- Financial interests drive decisions

**Ask yourself:** Have I made a decision this month that was not in my
own interest but in the interest of the collective or the client?

---

### 04 · Diversity & Inclusion

> *Different ways of thinking are not a problem to be solved – they are the strength of this collective.*

**Rooted in:** Lua Chea · CNCF · Open Source

The CNCF Code of Conduct explicitly names neurodiversity alongside age, gender,
and ethnicity as protected dimensions. The Cloud Native community – like open
source generally – attracts neurodiverse thinkers disproportionately. We do not
treat this as a challenge. We treat it as a design feature.

Neurodiversity in Business is not a social programme at the Open Ecosystem
Collective. It is a core competency.

**What it looks like when it is lived:**
- Neurodiversity is recognised as competence, not challenge
- Different communication styles are understood and accommodated
- Entry is possible at every maturity level
- No cultural, linguistic, or geographic barrier to participation
- Feedback processes are designed so everyone can participate

**What it looks like when it is missing:**
- "The way we communicate is the standard" – others must adapt
- Neurodiversity labelled as "difficult"
- Entry only possible for experienced people – Level 1 treated as inferior
- English as an implicit requirement without alternative
- Meetings and formats always following the same pattern

**Ask yourself:** Is there someone in the collective whose strengths
I do not yet fully understand or recognise?

---

### 05 · Courage

> *We say what is true even when it is uncomfortable. We start before we are ready. We give feedback even when it creates friction.*

**Rooted in:** Lua Chea · Open Source

Neurodiversity in Business means talking openly about neurodiversity in a world
that does not. Open source means publishing your work before it is perfect and
accepting criticism publicly. Apache's Lazy Consensus model makes this structural:
if you do not speak, you agree. Dissent requires words.

**What it looks like when it is lived:**
- Problems are addressed directly, not discussed behind backs
- Clients hear what they do not want to hear
- Methodology is published before it is perfect
- Disagreement is stated clearly – with reasoning
- Mistakes are communicated immediately and directly

**What it looks like when it is missing:**
- Problems avoided to preserve harmony
- Clients receive only positive feedback
- Publication waits until everything is perfect – which never comes
- Disagreements are swallowed
- Mistakes are communicated late or buried in diplomatic language

**Ask yourself:** Have I stated an opinion in the last 30 days
that was uncomfortable – and said it anyway?

---

### 06 · Reciprocity

> *What you take, you give back – not immediately, not the same amount, but consciously and continuously.*

**Rooted in:** Lua Chea · Open Source

Lua Chea's model: those who participate today make it possible for someone
else to participate tomorrow. Open source: bug reports, documentation,
code reviews – contribution without direct return. The CNCF End User
Community gives feedback back to the projects it depends on.
All three are the same pattern.

**What it looks like when it is lived:**
- Advancing to Level 2 means mentoring someone at Level 1
- Clients who grew return and refer others
- Improved methodology is contributed back to GitHub
- Good experiences with vendors are shared with the community
- Feedback given to the collective after every mandate

**What it looks like when it is missing:**
- "I learned it, it is mine now"
- Clients are not contacted after the project ends
- Improvements kept internally
- Experiences hoarded
- No feedback – positive or negative

**Ask yourself:** What have I given back this week – to the community,
to a member, to a client?

---

### 07 · Integrity

> *We do what we say. We say what we do. Also when no one is watching.*

**Rooted in:** All three worlds

Lua Chea's non-profit status: every franc goes to the purpose, verifiably.
CNCF's open governance: decisions are public, traceable, auditable.
Open source licences: a promise that is kept regardless of who is watching.
The same standard applies here.

**What it looks like when it is lived:**
- Commitments are kept or communicated early when they cannot be
- Revenue split is paid exactly as documented
- Client data remains confidential and is never used elsewhere
- Assessment results are honest even when uncomfortable
- The CC-BY-SA licence is respected in adapted versions

**What it looks like when it is missing:**
- "That was not in writing" used as an excuse
- Revenue split adjusted after the fact
- Client information shared in other conversations
- Reports softened to secure the next engagement
- CC-BY-SA ignored when reusing the methodology

**Ask yourself:** If someone reviewed my work today –
would I want them to see it?

## The values check

Five situations. Three possible responses each.  
No right answers. But clear patterns.

Used in the conversation before someone joins the collective.

---

**01** · You have developed a methodology that worked well in a client project. What do you do?

- **A** – Document it, share it with the collective, push it to GitHub
- **B** – Share it internally but keep it for your own external positioning
- **C** – Use it as a differentiator for your own brand

---

**02** · A client asks about a technology you do not know well, but another collective member knows it better. What do you do?

- **A** – Recommend the other member directly – even if it means losing the engagement
- **B** – Ask the other member if you can pitch together
- **C** – Take the engagement and learn fast

---

**03** · You notice that a collective member's client report is too positive and omits important problems. What do you do?

- **A** – Speak to the member directly – friendly but clear
- **B** – Mention it at the next collective meeting
- **C** – Say nothing – it is not your mandate

---

**04** · A new Level 1 member asks for help with a discovery conversation. You are busy. What happens?

- **A** – Find 30 minutes – investing in others is investing in the collective
- **B** – Send relevant documents and stay reachable via chat
- **C** – Explain you have no capacity and hope they find help elsewhere

---

**05** · You learn that another member contacted a collective client directly without informing anyone. What do you do?

- **A** – Raise it at the next collective meeting – without accusation, with a question
- **B** – Speak to the member privately
- **C** – Keep it to yourself – you prefer to avoid conflict

**Reading the pattern:**
- Mostly A → Values are already lived. Welcome.
- Mix of A and B → Right mindset, some patterns to develop. Good candidate.
- Mostly C → An honest conversation is needed first.
