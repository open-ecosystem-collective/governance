# Membership

**Open Ecosystem Collective – How to Join and What It Means**

Membership in the collective is not a title. It is a commitment.  
To the values. To the community. To the work.

**Version:** 1.0  
**License:** CC-BY-SA 4.0

## Who this is for

The Open Ecosystem Collective is for people who have experience in
partner ecosystem management – or a strong foundation to grow into it –
and who want to do that work within a values-based, transparent community
rather than alone.

**You might be:**
- A partner manager at a Cloud Native or Open Source company
  looking to go independent
- A freelance consultant who wants the credibility of a collective
  and access to a shared methodology
- Someone between roles who wants to build structured experience
  in partner ecosystem work
- An engineer who has been doing informal partner work and wants
  to formalise and grow that capability

**You do not need to be:**
- Already at full capacity
- Based in Switzerland or the DACH region
- A partner manager by title – contribution counts more than job history

## Membership types

### Collective Member
Full participation. Mandates, revenue split, peer review, voting rights.

This is the core membership. You deliver client work under the OEC methodology,
you contribute to the community, and you grow through the maturity levels.

**Revenue:** 70% of mandate value goes to you directly.

### Community Member
Observer and contributor. No mandates, no revenue split, full access
to methodology and governance documents.

Community members can open issues, submit pull requests, and participate
in governance discussions. They cannot vote on collective decisions or
receive client mandates.

Community membership is a natural entry point for people who want to
understand the collective before committing to full membership.

**Cost:** Free.

## How to become a Collective Member

### Step 1 – Read the foundations

Before reaching out, read:
- [`values-charter.md`](values-charter.md) – all of it
- [`maturity-levels.md`](maturity-levels.md) – especially your likely starting level
- [`decision-making.md`](decision-making.md) – how the collective works

If anything feels wrong or unclear, that is worth a conversation
before going further.

### Step 2 – Open an issue

Go to the [`governance` repository](https://github.com/open-ecosystem-collective/governance)
and open an issue with the title:

```
Membership interest – [Your name]
```

Include:
- A brief description of your background (3–5 sentences)
- Which maturity level you think fits your current experience and why
- What you want to contribute to the collective beyond client work
- One concrete example of partner ecosystem work you have done

There is no template. Write it the way you would write to a colleague,
not the way you would write a job application.

### Step 3 – Values conversation

Tania will reach out to schedule a 45-minute conversation.
This is not an interview. It is a conversation about the values –
using the situations from the [Values Charter](values-charter.md#the-values-check)
as a starting point.

The goal is to understand whether the values are already lived
or still on the way. Both can lead to membership – the difference
is in timing and starting level.

### Step 4 – Peer review (for Levels 2 and above)

If you are joining at Level 2 or above, two existing members at
the same level or higher will review your background and the
mandate examples you provided.

This is not gatekeeping. It is how we maintain the quality
that makes the collective's work credible.

### Step 5 – Lazy Consensus

Your membership is proposed to the collective.  
72-hour Lazy Consensus period.  
If no objection, you are in.

### Step 6 – Onboarding

- Added to the collective's shared channels and tools
- Assigned a buddy (an existing member at the same level)
- First mandate opportunity within 60 days where possible

## What membership requires

### Ongoing commitments

**Deliver with quality**  
Every mandate reflects on the collective. Deliver what you promise.
Communicate early if something is not going as expected.

**Document your work**  
Completed mandates go into the shared record (anonymised client details).
At least one case study per maturity level, published in the methodology repository.

**Participate in peer review**  
When asked to review another member's advancement, respond within 7 days.

**Give back**  
When you advance to a new level, commit to mentoring one person
at the level you just left. This is not optional.

**Stay current**  
Read governance updates. Participate in collective decisions.
Silence in the 72h window is a vote. Make it a conscious one.

### What we do not require

- Full-time availability
- Minimum number of mandates per year
- Geographic presence in Switzerland
- Any specific technology stack or certification

## Leaving the collective

### Voluntary departure

Open an issue in the governance repository or message Tania directly.  
There is no notice period required. Ongoing mandates must be
completed or formally handed over before departure.

Departed members may return. The values conversation happens again.
Previous maturity level may be reinstated after peer review.

### Involuntary departure

If a member consistently acts against the collective's values,
the following process applies:

1. Direct conversation with the member (Courage, value 05)
2. If unresolved: peer review by two members and Tania
3. Outcome: return to good standing, step down one level, or departure
4. Decision documented in CHANGELOG.md (without personal details)

This process exists to protect the collective and the member equally.
It is not used lightly.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I work with clients outside the collective while being a member?**  
Yes. Membership does not require exclusivity. The only requirement is
that work brought through the collective's network uses the collective's
methodology and follows the revenue split.

**What if I want to start at Level 1 but have more experience?**  
You can. Some people choose to start at a lower level deliberately –
to learn the methodology properly before moving up. That is a
legitimate and respected choice.

**What language do we work in?**  
English and German. Client work happens in whatever language the
client needs. Internal collective communication is primarily English
to keep it accessible to all members.

**Is there a membership fee?**  
No. The collective's sustainability comes from the 20% of mandate
revenue that flows to the foundation (Lua Chea). Community members pay nothing.
